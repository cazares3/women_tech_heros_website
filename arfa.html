Background

Arfa Karim was born on February 2, 1995 in Faisalabad, Pakistan. She was the only child of Abdul Karim Randhawa, a retired officer of the United Nations, and Samina Karim, a housewife. She belonged to an ethnic Punjabi family from Ram Diwali village in Faisalabad district. She attended Lahore Grammar School and Beaconhouse School System, where she excelled in academics and extracurricular activities. She had a keen interest in mathematics, science, and technology from an early age. She also enjoyed poetry, music, and chess.

Contributions

Arfa Karim is best known for becoming the youngest Microsoft Certified Professional (MCP) in 2004, at the age of nine. She achieved this feat by passing the Microsoft exam 70-210: Installing, Configuring, and Administering Microsoft Windows 2000 Professional. She was submitted to the Guinness Book of World Records for her achievement. She also received a letter of appreciation from Bill Gates, the founder of Microsoft, who invited her to visit the Microsoft headquarters in the United States in 2005. She met Gates and other Microsoft executives and employees, and discussed various topics related to technology and education.

Arfa Karim also represented Pakistan on various international forums and platforms, such as the TechEd Developers Conference in Barcelona in 2006, where she was the keynote speaker. She also participated in the Microsoft Imagine Cup competition in 2010, where she presented a project on rural agriculture development using technology. She also received numerous awards and honors from the government and private sector of Pakistan, such as the Fatima Jinnah Gold Medal, the Salaam Pakistan Youth Award, and the President's Award for Pride of Performance. She was also made the brand ambassador for Pakistan Telecommunication Company's 3G Wireless Broadband service, "EVO", in 2010.

Challenges

Arfa Karim faced many challenges and difficulties in her short but remarkable life. She had to cope with the pressure and expectations of being a child prodigy and a national icon. She also had to balance her studies, hobbies, and social life with her passion for technology and innovation. She also had to deal with health problems, such as epilepsy and cardiac issues, that affected her work and life. She died on January 14, 2012, at the age of 16, after suffering a cardiac arrest due to an epileptic seizure. She was admitted to the Combined Military Hospital Lahore for almost a month before her death. Her funeral was attended by thousands of people, including dignitaries, celebrities, students, and fans. She was buried in her ancestral village of Ram Diwali.
