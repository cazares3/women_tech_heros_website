Background

Safiya Umoja Noble was born on January 6, 1976 in Los Angeles, California. She is of African American and Puerto Rican descent. She grew up in a working-class family and attended public schools. She earned her bachelor's degree in sociology from California State University, Fresno, her master's degree in library and information science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, and her PhD in information studies from the University of California, Los Angeles.

Safiya Umoja Noble is currently an associate professor at the University of California, Los Angeles, in the departments of information studies and African American studies. She is also a co-founder and co-director of the UCLA Center for Critical Internet Inquiry, a research center that aims to advance racial and economic justice in the digital age. She is also a faculty associate at the Berkman Klein Center for Internet and Society at Harvard University, and a board member of the Cyber Civil Rights Initiative.

Contributions

Safiya Umoja Noble is best known for her work on algorithmic bias, or how algorithms and search engines can reproduce and reinforce social inequalities and discriminations. She is the author of the award-winning book Algorithms of Oppression: How Search Engines Reinforce Racism, published in 2018. In this book, she exposes how Google and other search engines can perpetuate harmful stereotypes and misrepresentations of marginalized groups, especially women of color. She also offers suggestions for how to challenge and change these practices.

Safiya Umoja Noble's work has influenced public policy, media, education, and activism. She has testified before the U.S. Congress on issues related to algorithmic accountability and transparency. She has also written op-eds and articles for popular outlets such as The Guardian, The New York Times, Time, Wired, etc. She has also given keynote speeches and lectures at various conferences and events around the world. She has also mentored and supported many students and scholars from underrepresented backgrounds.

Challenges

Safiya Umoja Noble has faced many challenges and barriers as a woman of color in academia and technology. She has experienced racism, sexism, harassment, and discrimination from peers, colleagues, reviewers, funders, etc. She has also faced resistance and backlash from powerful corporations and institutions that benefit from the status quo. She has also had to balance her personal and professional life as a mother, wife, daughter, sister, friend, etc.
